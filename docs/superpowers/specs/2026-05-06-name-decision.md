# Name Decision: Fumletone

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Status:** Locked in (pending user-side availability checks)

## Decision

The app will be named **Fumletone**.

## Context

A music-theory app for children, primarily intended for the author's two children
(ages 7 and 10) on iPad. The name needed to:

- Work equally well in Norwegian and English
- Feel playful and magical
- Be available (no significant brand or app collisions)

## Rationale

- **Cross-lingual resonance.** Norwegian *fumle* and English *fumble* are
  essentially the same word, both meaning "to fiddle around clumsily but
  harmlessly." This is exactly what beginning music students do, in the most
  affectionate sense. Children, parents, and teachers in both languages will
  intuit the tone immediately.
- **Product positioning.** The name promises a low-pressure, playful approach
  to learning — a deliberate counterpoint to the drill-and-score gamification
  common in the kids-music-app category.
- **Form.** *Fumle-* (playful, character-suggesting) + *-tone* (grounding the
  app in music). Three syllables, easy to pronounce in both languages.
- **Tone caveat.** English *fumble* carries a faint negative shade (clumsiness,
  sports). The visual brand should lean into a bumbling, friendly mascot finding
  music by accident — not a perfectionist judge. This matches the intended
  encouraging feel.

## Alternatives Considered

The brainstorm explored four directions:

1. **Bell/chime sounds + magical suffix** (e.g. Plingo, Klingli, Plinka)
2. **Music root + invented ending** (e.g. Notimo, Trillito, Tonika)
3. **Pure nonsense singing syllables** (e.g. Lirumi, Tralalu, Bimbam)
4. **Two-part compounds** (e.g. Frumlebass, Dindrabox)

**Plinka** was the original favorite but ruled out due to direct collisions:
plinkamusic.com is an active site, and *Plinka Plinka Shake Shake* is a
children's musical-instruments board book by Emma Garcia — same audience, same
category.

**Frumletone, Frumleland, Frumlepark** were finalists with clean availability
checks. Fumletone was preferred over Frumletone for its true cross-lingual
meaning anchor (fumle/fumble), which Frumle lacked.

**Dindrabeat** was ruled out because "Dindra" is an active electronic-music
artist on Beatport/Spotify/Apple Music — direct search collision.

## Availability Check (assistant-side)

- Web search: zero direct hits for "Fumletone"
- App Store / Google Play: no app of this name
- USPTO trademark: no match
- Closest soft collision: *Fulmicotone* (Italian for guncotton; an Italian
  art/music project) — different word, different language, no overlap
